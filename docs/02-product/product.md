# 📦 APPRIYO WEBSITE — PRODUCT DEFINITION DOCUMENT (v1.0)

> **Purpose:**
> This document defines what the Appriyo website IS as a product — not as "a website."
> It defines the job it performs, the users it serves, the actions it drives, and how success is measured.
> All page design, content, and development decisions MUST align with this document.

---

# 1. 🎯 WHAT IS THIS WEBSITE'S JOB?

The Appriyo website is NOT a digital brochure.
The Appriyo website is NOT a portfolio showcase.

**The website is a trust-building and lead-conversion machine for SMB clients.**

### One-Line Product Definition

> **The Appriyo website exists to convert business owners who have a manual workflow problem into qualified leads who book a consultation call.**

Every section, every word, every button exists for this one outcome.

# 3. 👤 TARGET USER TYPES (VISITOR PERSONAS)

## Persona 1 — The Manual Business Owner (PRIMARY)

**Who:**
- Runs a small shop, service center, or local business
- 1–10 employees
- Uses pen-and-paper, WhatsApp, or Excel for operations
- Not technical — but frustrated by inefficiency

**What they feel:**
- "I'm wasting too much time on repetitive tasks"
- "I don't know where to start with technology"
- "I'm afraid it'll be too complex or too expensive"

**What they need from the site:**
- Simple language (no jargon)
- Proof that this is made for businesses like theirs
- A low-friction way to ask for help

**Desired action:**
→ Fill the contact/consultation form

---

## Persona 2 — The Early-Stage Startup Founder (SECONDARY)

**Who:**
- Building a product or service idea
- Needs automation or a custom software system
- Somewhat technical but not a developer

**What they need from the site:**
- Clarity on what Appriyo actually builds
- Confidence in the team's technical capability
- Portfolio or proof of real work

**Desired action:**
→ Contact for a custom software quote

---

## Persona 3 — The Researcher / Evaluator (PASSIVE)

**Who:**
- Comparing agencies or freelancers
- May refer Appriyo to someone else
- Investor, potential partner, or journalist

**What they need from the site:**
- Clear identity (what Appriyo is)
- Team credibility
- Product evidence

**Desired action:**
→ Save contact, follow on LinkedIn, or refer

# 5. 🔑 CONVERSION POINTS (WHERE VISITORS BECOME LEADS)

| Location                               | CTA Text                         | Action                     |
|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Hero Section (above fold)              | "Let's Talk About Your Business" | Scrolls to / opens Contact |
| Services Section (end)                 | "Get a Free Consultation"        | Scrolls to Contact         |
| Solutions Section (each solution card) | "This Is My Problem →"           | Scrolls to Contact         |
| Products Section                       | "See How It Works"               | Links to product sub-page  |
| Why Appriyo Section                    | "Start with a Free Call"         | Scrolls to Contact         |
| Sticky Navbar (desktop)                | "Contact Us"                     | Scrolls to Contact         |
| Footer                                 | "Book a Consultation"            | Links to Contact page      |

### Consultation Form Fields (Minimal Friction)

The contact form must ask ONLY:
1. Name
2. Business Type (dropdown: Repair Shop / Coaching / Retail / Startup / Other)
3. Current Problem (textarea — 2–3 sentences)
4. WhatsApp / Phone Number
5. Preferred Contact Method (WhatsApp / Email / Call)

> ❌ Do NOT ask: budget, timeline, technical specs upfront.
> The first goal is just to start the conversation.

# 7. 📋 CONTENT STRATEGY

## Messaging Hierarchy

Every page section follows this order:

1. **Problem statement first** — "Are you still doing X manually?"
2. **Outcome promise second** — "Appriyo replaces that with Y"
3. **Proof third** — Product / approach / team
4. **Action fourth** — CTA

## Tone of Voice

| Attribute      | Description                                                                |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Language level | Simple, clear — no jargon                                                  |
| Tone           | Calm, direct, confident — not salesy                                       |
| Perspective    | "We understand your problem" — empathetic                                  |
| Avoid          | Tech buzzwords, vague phrases like "synergy", "innovation", "cutting-edge" |

## What the Website Will NOT Say

- ❌ "We are a leading software company" (vague, untrustworthy)
- ❌ "We use the latest technologies" (meaningless)
- ❌ "End-to-end solutions" (jargon)
- ❌ "Transform your digital future" (abstract)

## What the Website WILL Say

- ✅ "Still tracking repairs on paper? There's a better way."
- ✅ "We build the system. You run your business."
- ✅ "No tech knowledge needed. We handle everything."
- ✅ "Real software for real small businesses."

# 8. 🧱 PAGE-LEVEL PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS

## 8.1 Homepage (`/`)

**Job:** Create instant recognition, build trust, and drive to Contact.

**Must achieve in 5 seconds:**
- Visitor understands what Appriyo does
- Visitor sees their problem reflected
- Visitor knows the next step

**Sections (in order):**
1. Hero — Problem statement + CTA
2. Services — 4 core services (cards)
3. Solutions — 3–5 real business problems Appriyo solves
4. Products — Amar Repair, Amar Batch, Amar Card (brief)
5. Why Appriyo — Trust signals
6. Our Approach — How Appriyo works
7. About / Team — Brief intro (not a full bio page)
8. Contact — Form (primary CTA destination)

---

## 8.2 Services Page (`/services`)

**Job:** Convince technically-curious visitors that Appriyo has the capability to solve their problem.

**Must achieve:**
- Clear explanation of each service
- Real examples / use cases
- CTA per service

---

## 8.3 Solutions Page (`/solutions`)

**Job:** Help visitors self-identify their problem and see a path to resolution.

**Format:** Problem → How Appriyo solves it → Result

**Example entries:**
- "Managing repair orders manually" → Amar Repair
- "Tracking student fees in notebooks" → Amar Batch
- "Losing business cards" → Amar Card

---

## 8.4 Products Section / Pages

**Job:** Demonstrate that Appriyo builds real, working products — not just services.

Each product page must include:
- What problem it solves
- Who it's for
- Core features (simple list)
- Screenshot / mockup (when available)
- CTA: "Interested? Let's talk"

---

## 8.5 About Page (`/about`)

**Job:** Build human trust. Show the people behind the company.

**Must include:**
- Company philosophy (from constitution §1.3)
- Team section (4 members with role + photo)
- What makes Appriyo different (not a generic list — real differentiators)

**Must NOT be:**
- A generic "Our Story" page with filler content

---

## 8.6 Contact Page (`/contact`)

**Job:** Make it as easy as possible to start a conversation.

**Must include:**
- Short form (fields listed in §5)
- WhatsApp direct link
- Email address
- Response time expectation: "We respond within 1 business day"
- Office hours

# 9. 🚫 WHAT THIS WEBSITE WILL NOT DO

# 11. ✅ PRODUCT DECISION FILTER

Before adding any new section, page, or feature to the website, ask:

1. Does it serve one of the 3 user personas?
2. Does it move a visitor closer to contacting Appriyo?
3. Does it build trust or clarify the problem we solve?
4. Does it align with the design system's simplicity rules?

👉 If 2 or more answers are NO → Do not build it.
